3.2 Encoding of UA-FLP on FBS with bay list
The FBS representation consists of two main parts. A
rectangular plant is divided in one direction into several
parallel bays, each with varying widths or heights. Withineach bay, multiple facilities of the same width or height are
arranged. The general encoding for FBS typically contains
two components: the facility sequence which includes thefacility numbers, and the bay division which consists of 1 s
or 0 s indicating whether a facility is the last one in its
corresponding bay.

4 Learning-based SA algorithm for UA-FLP
with FBS
The following section elaborates the process of LSA. The
primary objective of LSA is to minimize the total cost of
material ﬂow between facilities. To achieve this goal, withthe aforementioned layout representation, an initial ﬂexible
solution is created using a greedy initialization strategy.
Within a fundamental SA framework, a set of neighbor-hood disturbing operators is utilized to perform an
exhaustive local search. In addition, a self-learningcontroller is employed to make the selection decision
during the search process. When a new solution is gener-
ated, if it is better than the current solution or meets
acceptance criteria, it is further accepted and explored witha greedy two-stage local search. This process continues
iteratively until a termination condition is met. Algo 2
outlines the general algorithm framework.

4.1 Greedy initial solution generation
For most meta-heuristics, the initial search typically beginswith a randomly generated solution. This approach aims to
provide an initial broad search space and hopes to ﬁnd
better solution in subsequent iteration. However, in the
case of UA-FLP, generating an initial solution can be
extremely time-consuming due to the stringent constraintsinvolved. In UA-FLP, each facility is subjects to either a
maximum proportion constraint or a minimum value con-
straint. Intuitively, smaller facilities are more likely toviolate these constraints when they are located in the same
bay as larger facilities.
Moreover, as the plant becomes ﬁlled with a couple of
facilities, it becomes more difﬁcult to add large-size
facilities due to the area constraint. To address these issues,
an area-prior greedy initial construction method is pro-posed to enhance the efﬁciency of initialization. The pro-
cess begins by sorting the facilities based on their areas to5672 J. Lin et al.

4.2 Neighborhood generators and learning
model
In local search, a widely used approach is to incorporate
multiple neighborhood generation strategies instead of justa single one. This allows for the utilization of various
beneﬁcial information from dynamically changing search
neighborhoods. In the context of handling the elastic searchspace, four operators are included: facility swap, bay swap,
bay ﬂip, and infeasible solution repair. Facility swap and
bay swap are general operators applicable to most algo-
rithms. These operators involve randomly selecting twoswapping points and modifying the corresponding facilities
or bays accordingly. Bay ﬂip is a novel operator speciﬁ-
cally designed for FBS with a bay list representation. Ini-tially, a random facility is selected. If this facility happens
to be the last one in its bay, it is detached from the current
bay and placed in a new bay, becoming the sole item in that
bay. On the other hand, if the facility is located in the
middle of the bay, all facilities following it are separatedfrom their bay and assigned a new bay. To increase the
